病史(Brief History) 
This 79-year-old woman has underlying disease of osteoarthritis of knee arthritis under celebrex, viartril and solaxin therapy.

She complained of chest tightness occasionally but improved after rest. She suffered from abdominal pain accompanied with vomiting and diarrhea after dinner since 12/8 night. Abdminal pain over epigastric area without peritoneal sign or radiation to back. Vomiting with clod sweating and chillness were noted. No fever or others discomfort were noted. Then she visited NTUH ED for help. 

At our ER, undetectable blood pressure and hypothermia were noted at triage. Arterial blood gas at ED showed metabolic acidosis. Blood test revealed hyperlactemia(7.57), leukocytosis(10k) and mild elevated Troponin I. Emergent C+A+P CT revealed right renal cyst, bilateral basal lung inflammation and mild gall bladder wall thickening with peripheral fluid accumulation. Cardioogist was consulted at ED to rule out acute coronary syndrome, myocardial injury secondary to septic shock was favored by coardiologist first. Consciousness change and dyspnea developed while received treatment at ED. Intubation was done and she was admitted to ICU for further evaluation and management.

She was under Invanz and Zithromax therapy for infection control. Inotropic agents of Dopamine, Levophed and Bosmine were given but hypotension persisted. Critical condition was informed and family requested aggressive treatment at that time. General surgeon was consulted and surgery is not suggested due to critical condition. 

However, followed up cardiac enzyme showed significant elevation since 12/9 11:45(CK 3101U/L, CK-MB 481U/L, troponin-I > 80ug/ml). Refractory hypotension and pink frothy sputum persisted. Cardiac specialist was consulted and cardiac echogragm showed decreased LVEF (16.7%, with anteroseptal+apex akinesia). Cardiogenic shock was impressed and IABP was suggested. However, PEA developed on 13:42 and CPCR was started. ECMO team was contacted and ECMO start running at 14:15. IABP was also inserted later.

Emergent PCI was performed, which showed LAD proximal total occlusion, LCX proximal to distal multiple stenosis up to 80%, RCA proximal to distal multiple stenosis up to 80%, with collaterals to LAD and LCX. Since PCI could not improve the total occlusion, CVS surgeon was consulted. After well explaining and discussing to his family, they refuse further surgical treatment and preferred palliative care. Then she was transferred to 5CVI for further management.
